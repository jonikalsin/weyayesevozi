    Mike Lindell's Political Connection With Donald Trump

    Mike Lindell met Trump in August 2016, while the former was the Republican presidential candidate, and quickly became a public supporter and major donor to his campaign, reports independent.co.uk.

    Lindell attended the final presidential debate in October 2016, before speaking at a campaign rally for Trump and watching election night coverage at the official Donald Watch Party on 8 November.

    At a rally for Trump following his 2016 presidential election victory, Lindell praised him, saying he will be 'the most amazing president this country has ever seen in history.'

    After attending Trump’s inauguration as a guest on 20 January 2017, the former president gave Lindell an inauguration lapel pin as a personal gift, according to the Minneapolis/St Paul Business Journal.

    Lindell was pictured sitting next to Trump at an industry roundtable event at the White House later that year and has continued to support the president at rallies in the years since.

    However, Trump and Lindell caused controversy in 2020, when the MyPillow CEO promoted a fake coronavirus cure in several meetings and public appearances with the president. 

    Lindell also appeared at numerous public events with Trump in 2020 and announced at a White House press briefing last March that he was converting MyPillow production to produce up to 50,000 face masks for health workers on the front lines of the pandemic. 

    Lindell has repeated conspiracy theories forwarded by Trump and his supporters, most recently falsely claiming that members of Antifa were behind the Capitol riots, 'dressed as Trump people.'
