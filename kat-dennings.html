    Bio:
    Kat Dennings (born Katherine Litwack on June 13, 1986 in Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania) is an American actress. Kat was homeschooled as a youth, graduating from high school early at age 14. By then, she had already begun her acting career, first appearing in commercials at the age of 10. She and her family relocated to Los Angeles, California, so she could pursue acting full-time. Her first professional debut came in 200, when she acted in an episode of Sex and the City. She starred on the short-lived WB sitcom, Raising Dad, featuring Bob Saget and Brie Larson. She had a recurring role on ER from 2005 to 2006 and appeared on CSI twice. Her feature film debut came in 2004, when she started opposite Hilary Duff in Raise Your Voice. She had supporting roles in The 40-Year-Old Virgin, Down In The Valley and Big Mamma’s House 2. Her role in Nick and Nora’s Infinite Playlist gained her critical acclaim. In 2011, she achieved a huge mainstream breakthrough, landing roles in the Marvel Studios movie Thor and being cast in the hit CBS sitcom, 2 Broke Girls.

    Best Known For:
    Kat Dennings is best known as an actress, appearing in movies like Thor, The 40-Year-Old Virgin and on shows like 2 Broke Girls.

    Personal Life:
    From 2001 to 2010, Kat was an active blogger before transitioning to video blogging on YouTube. In 2008, she said she doesn’t smoke, drink or hang out with people who do. While claiming that Judaism is an important part of her history, Kat admits that she’s not religious. In 2014, she began dating singer Josh Groban. The two announced their split in Aug. 2016.
